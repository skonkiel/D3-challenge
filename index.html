Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Income

Recent analysis of the 2014 ACS 1-year estimates (released by the US government) show a direct relationship between average rates of healthcare coverage and poverty level throughout the United States.

NPR reports that despite small decreases in the poverty rate in recent years, "the bureau reported separately that the number of people in the U.S. who did not have health insurance rose from 25.6 million people in 2017 to 27.5 million in 2018. That included 4.3 million children."
